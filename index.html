Overview

What this project is about

I used the COVID-19 World Vaccination Progress dataset from Kaggle, which covers daily vaccination records across 223 countries from December 2020 to March 2022. The dataset had 86,512 rows and around 50% of the total_vaccinations column was missing, which is pretty common with COVID data since not every country reported daily.

The main questions I wanted to answer: which countries vaccinated the most people, which vaccines dominated globally, and how did the rollout actually look over time across major countries including Canada.

🌍
Raw numbers favour large countries. China leads with 1.27 billion vaccinated and India follows with 984 million. But this is mostly a reflection of population size, not vaccination efficiency.
📊
Gibraltar vaccinated 124% of its population. When you adjust for population size, small territories top the list. Gibraltar exceeded 100% by vaccinating cross-border workers and visitors alongside residents. Niue and Pitcairn also exceeded 100%.
💉
Pfizer/BioNTech dominated globally. With 600 million doses, Pfizer was nearly 3 times more widely used than Moderna in second place at 210 million. AstraZeneca came third at 67 million.
🇨🇦
Canada had one of the strongest rollouts. Despite starting later than the US and UK, Canada caught up quickly and ended with the highest vaccination rate among the 5 countries compared at 85% by March 2022.
⚠️
Data quality was a real issue. Around 50% of the total_vaccinations column had missing values. This is worth keeping in mind when interpreting any results from this dataset.
